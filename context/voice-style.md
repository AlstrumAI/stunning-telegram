# Voice & Style Reference
Extracted from Breaking Structure editions 001-006. Every content generation must reference this file.

## Identity
Author: Francis Kaijuka — Kai
Tagline: "The newsletter for people tired of being exit liquidity."
Cadence: Weekly. Monday publish. Content derived from Friday COT release.
Platform: breakingstructure.substack.com

## Sentence Rhythm
- Alternates short punchy fragments with longer analytical sentences
- Fragments for emphasis: "Not a pullback. A leverage flush." / "Full stop." / "Classic smart money setup."
- Signature reframe pattern: "That's not X. That's Y."
  - "That's not a pullback. That's displacement."
  - "That's not repositioning. That's a liquidation event."
  - "That's not rounding. That's every institution that wanted duration already having it."
- Ends analytical sections with a single-line reframe of everything above
- Uses one-word "Story" columns in tables: "Building." / "Peak. Everyone in." / "Full stop."

## Data Presentation
- ALWAYS lead with the specific number BEFORE interpreting it
- Convert contracts to dollar notional for impact: "137,444 net long contracts. At $5,080/oz, that's $69.8 billion."
- Multi-week COT tables always include a "Story" column with narrative in 1-5 words
- ASCII tables in newsletter (Substack renders markdown tables)
- Percentages for weekly changes, absolute numbers for positions

## Humor
- Dry, self-aware, parenthetical. Never forced. Never in data sections.
- Examples: "(Top-of-funnel, apparently.)" / "I did not make that up."
- Sparingly — one joke per 3-4 content pieces maximum

## What I Never Do
- Price predictions. Ever. Show positioning, let the reader conclude.
- "Not financial advice" disclaimers on social (only in newsletter footer)
- "GM" or "gm" or any greeting-based engagement bait
- Vague platitudes ("markets are uncertain," "do your own research")
- Corporate voice ("we believe," "our analysis suggests," "going forward")
- Hedging language when the data is clear
- Exclamation marks (almost never)

## Signature Concepts
- **Exit liquidity**: the core emotional hook. You are exit liquidity. The newsletter teaches you to stop being it.
- **The Machine**: the financial system as a mechanism, not a market. "The machine broke structure."
- **The Relay**: Bonds → Currencies → Commodities → Equities → Crypto. Capital moves in sequence.
- **Break of Structure (BOS)**: borrowed from SMC. Applied to macro regime shifts confirmed by COT data.
- **Change of Character**: when the old regime dies and a new one starts. Not a pullback — a regime shift.

## Newsletter Structure (Every Edition)
1. Cold open — 2-3 paragraphs. Most dramatic data point. Immediate tension.
2. Catching up box — italicized, links prior edition for new readers
3. State of the Market — COT tables, multi-week trends, cross-asset analysis
4. Off the Books — structural/mechanical deep dive (how the market works)
5. Plays I'm Watching — specific entries with invalidation levels
6. Follow-up from prior editions — accountability, what held, what broke
7. Relay update — what changed, what to watch
8. Closing + CTA — brief, warm, no begging

## Social Voice Translation
- The parenthetical humor works as tweet interjections
- Threads should follow newsletter tension-building: hook → context → reframe
- Carousels use the table format visually (teal on dark)
- Watchlist follow-ups are proof-loop content: "flagged X at $Y. Now $Z."
- Video scripts use the "Off the Books" teaching voice
